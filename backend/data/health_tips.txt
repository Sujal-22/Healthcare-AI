GENERAL HEALTH TIPS AND GUIDELINES

NUTRITION
A balanced diet should include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
Adults should aim for at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables daily.
Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive sodium intake.
Stay hydrated — drink 8-10 glasses of water per day.
Avoid skipping breakfast as it boosts metabolism and concentration.

EXERCISE
Adults should get at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week.
Include strength training exercises at least 2 days per week.
Even 30 minutes of brisk walking daily significantly reduces health risks.
Stretching and flexibility exercises help prevent injury and improve mobility.
Consult a doctor before starting a new exercise program if you have chronic conditions.

SLEEP
Adults need 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night.
Maintain a consistent sleep schedule even on weekends.
Avoid screens (phones, tablets, TV) at least 1 hour before bed.
Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet for better sleep.
Chronic sleep deprivation increases risk of obesity, diabetes, and heart disease.

MENTAL HEALTH
Manage stress through meditation, deep breathing, or yoga.
Maintain social connections — loneliness increases health risks.
Take breaks during work to prevent burnout.
Seek professional help if experiencing persistent anxiety or depression.
Limit alcohol consumption and avoid recreational drugs.

PREVENTIVE CARE
Schedule regular health checkups even when feeling well.
Keep vaccinations up to date — flu shot annually, tetanus every 10 years.
Monitor blood pressure regularly — normal is below 120/80 mmHg.
Adults over 45 should get blood sugar tested every 3 years.
Women should get regular mammograms and Pap smears as recommended by their doctor.
Men over 50 should discuss prostate cancer screening with their doctor.

FIRST AID BASICS
For minor cuts: clean with water, apply antiseptic, cover with bandage.
For burns: cool with running water for 10-20 minutes, do not use ice or butter.
For choking: perform Heimlich maneuver — stand behind person, give abdominal thrusts.
For heart attack symptoms (chest pain, arm pain, shortness of breath): call emergency services immediately.
For allergic reactions with swelling of throat or difficulty breathing: use epinephrine if available and call emergency.

MEDICATION SAFETY
Always complete the full course of antibiotics even if you feel better.
Never share prescription medications with others.
Store medications away from heat and moisture (not in bathrooms).
Check expiry dates before taking any medication.
Tell your doctor about all supplements and over-the-counter drugs you take to avoid interactions.

DISCLAIMER
This information is for educational purposes only and does not replace professional medical advice.
Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment of medical conditions.
In case of emergency, call your local emergency services immediately.
